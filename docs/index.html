Analyzing Climate Change Impact on Vegetation Dynamics in the Four Corners Region

A Detailed Analysis of Climate Change Impact from 1980 - 2024

Introduction

"Climate change is the greatest threat to our existence in our short history on this planet. Nobody's going to buy their way out of its effects."

The Four Corners is a region of the Southwestern United States consisting of the southwestern corner of Colorado, southeastern corner of Utah, northeastern corner of Arizona, and northwestern corner of New Mexico. This project aims to analyze and visualize the impacts of climate change on this region over the years, using comprehensive climate data. This dataset consists of crucial climate variables such as temperature and precipitation across different seasons. I am going to utilise these climate variables to analyse the change in patterns in vegetation such as increase or decrease in percentage coverage of bare ground, tree canopy, herbs, shrubs and plant litter.The general hypotheses is that the Four Corners region, particularly the Natural Bridges National Monument in Utah, has experienced various changes in vegetation over the past decades. I will display a visual analysis aiming to explore the changes using vegetation metrics from 1980 to 2024 to either accept or reject the hypotheses.

About the Data

I have merged the two datasets :- historical and nearterm dataset. The historical dataset ranges from 1980 - 2018 and the Nearterm dataset ranges from 2021-2024. The data consists of 29 columns of which the key variables are Temperature, Precipitation, Volumetric Water Content (VWC), and vegetation metrics like tree canopy, herb cover, and bare ground.

Long and Lat

The longitude and latitude of the datapoint

Year and Time Period

Year is the range from 1980-2018 and 2021-2024 and time period is categorical(Hist and NT)

RCP and scenario

Scenarios that include assumptions about future economic activity

Vegetation Metrics

Percentage of tree canopy, herbs, shrubs and bare coverage.

Temperature

Temperature in winter, summer and annually.

Precipitation

Precipitation in winter, summer and annually.

VWC

Volumetric water content for all 4 seasons.

Soil Profile

Dry and Non dry soil days in the soil profile during summer.

Process

Data Merging and Cleaning

There were 2 datasets available which I merged into a single file. There were extensive missing values in the dataset which I imputed using groupby for year and scenario and filling the NA values with average, max and min values.

Feature Engineering

Extensively studied the structure of the data and the relationship of one variable with the other. Performed correlation analysis and used heatmaps to study this time series data and analyse the vegetation cover.

Visual Analysis

Finally, the data was analyzed using different methods of Data Visualizations tools such as Plotly. The inferences from these plots has helped me to understand the impact of climate change on vegetation cover better.

Conclusions and Inferences

From the above visual analysis, we can infer that the tree canopy in the Four Corners region, particularly in the Natural Bridges National Monument in Utah, has experienced significant changes from 1980 to 2024. The significant decreases in tree canopy coverage from 1982 to 1985 and from 2017 to 2023 can be attributed to environmental factors such as harsh winters and possibly increased human activity resulting in deforestation for warmth or other needs. Additionally, the increase in bare ground coverage during these periods supports the hypothesis that tree cutting could be a contributing factor. The climate chart further supports this by showing a noticeable pattern of extreme temperatures during the years of significant canopy decrease. The volumetric water content in the soil profile also indicates that water availability varies seasonally, which could impact vegetation health and growth. This study highlights the importance of monitoring and managing tree canopy and vegetation metrics to understand the impacts of climate change on ecosystems. The findings can inform conservation strategies to mitigate the adverse effects of climate change and preserve the natural habitats in the region.
